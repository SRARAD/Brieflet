Presentation Accessories

If you want to directly project from your Android™ tablet you may need a cable or other accessories. We've outlined some of the options below that we've tested. For your convenience, we've included photos and links to some specific products we've tested.

How can I connect my Android™ tablet to a projector or external display?

Projecting Digitally

If your projector/TV has a digital HDMI input and your tablet has an mini-HDMI out jack (like the Motorola Xoom) you can connect directly from the tablet to the projector with a mini-HDMI to HDMI cable.

Projecting with VGA

If your projector/TV has a VGA input you'll need to convert the digital signal from your device to the analog VGA signal. This is straight-forward but requires a converter. The converter may also give you direct access to the audio output of your tablet for connecting speakers.

The SIIG HDMI to VGA conversion box shown below works well with the Xoom.

Note: best results are achieved if you sleep the tablet (tap the power button to make the tablet screen go black), then connect to the converter/projector, then unsleep the tablet (by tapping the power button again) to sync to the display. Also make sure the output toggle button on the SIIG converter is in the VGA position (depressed). If you have it in the wrong position the output may have a green cast because it's in the wrong output mode.

Sound

You may want to ensure you have ability to play sound while connected to HDMI for display. Brieflet utilizes sound in video playback and potentially applications that you are linking to from your presentation may include sound. If you're using a VGA conversion box you can connect speakers to the mini-jack present on some of the converters. Another nice option is to use one of the wireless bluetooth speakers. Some are rechargeable, will automatically pair as a bluetooth speaker, can function as a full speakerphone when desired, and offer manual mini-jack cabling if you prefer to connect directly to a dock or VGA converter.

Can I use presentation remotes?

Yes, there are a couple of ways to control Brieflet by remote currently under Honeycomb 3.1.

Bluetooth HID Devices

One of the easiest ways to remote control your Android™ tablet is through use of bluetooth HID support on Honeycomb 3.1 and later devices. Most bluetooth mice can be paired with your tablet and will allow you full control of the Brieflet application wirelessly (and everything else on the tablet). Brieflet will also work with bluetooth keyboards. In presentation mode, use right arrow to advance to the next slide, left arrow to move to the previous slide, and up arrow to move to the grid view. In other views, the arrow keys move the focus and enter can be used to select.

Presentation Remote With Host Mode USB

Brieflet also supports some presentation remotes using Honeycomb 3.1 and later host-mode USB support. These remotes often have a built-in laser pointer and various control buttons. Typically, buttons might exist for previous slide, next slide, screen blanking, and restarting the presentation. Brieflet provides support for these actions when in the full-screen slide view. In order to use this kind of remote you'll need both a host-mode USB cable and a presentation remote.
